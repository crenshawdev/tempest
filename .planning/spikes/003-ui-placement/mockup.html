Pros: reuses the existing AQI summary-row idiom exactly. Zero new vocabulary. Dominant active species front and center; "1 other active" caption hints at olive without taking another row. Lowest-effort visual integration.

Pros: the most information-dense option that still respects the Current tab. Zero clicks to see active species + severity + raw value. "Showing N of 6 active" pre-empts the "where are the other species?" question.
Cons: grows the Current tab vertically. With 2 active species the card is ~80px tall; with 4 it would be 160px. Spike 001 shows 3+ active is rare but not impossible (peak grass + late spring olive + early ragweed in southern Italy late June, plausibly). A second large card under AQI on a 480px×~600px popup starts to push the alerts / settings rows below the fold.
Hidden cost: there's no symmetry with AQI. AQI gets a one-row summary + drill-down; pollen would get a multi-row inline expansion. Different shapes for similar things is design debt.
VERDICT
Variant B (drill-down) wins. It is the only option that (a) preserves visual symmetry with the existing AQI pattern, (b) leaves room to show all six species + off-season state, (c) keeps the Current tab compact, and (d) handles the 1-active and 4-active extremes without growing the layout. Variant A is the runner-up if a single-row summary is enough and we accept never showing more than one species in the popup proper. Variant C is rejected: same information density as B at the cost of breaking the AQI-style summary symmetry.

Recommended summary-row content: show the *highest* active species and its category, with a caption counting the others. Example: "Grass Moderate · 1 other active". If no species is active anywhere, suppress the row entirely (don't show "Off season" as a state — it's noise).

Out of scope for the spike (decisions deferred to the build phase): exact pill colors, whether to use a dedicated pollen icon vs. text, exact Fluent wording for "N other active," and whether the drill-down lives on its own `showing_pollen` flag or shares state with `showing_pollutants`.
